## Executive Summary

Yentl has the bones of a sharp product voice: serious, live, skeptical, and useful. The best lines already sound like a real product, especially:

- "Heads up - recording the people around you may need their consent. Yentl doesn't know where you are; you do."
- "End this session?"
- "Save this session"
- "Caption import stopped"
- "Waiting for tab audio"

But the app currently speaks in several conflicting voices:

1. Polished public-product voice: calm, confident, privacy-aware.
2. Internal QA voice: "proven replay", "functional samples", "Open real test page", "validation fixture".
3. Engineering/provider voice: "Deepgram", "server is not configured", "engagement gate", "attach".
4. AI-marketing voice: "surfaces the biases and fallacies tucked into the rhetoric", "evidence anchors", "No false or misleading claim has been surfaced yet."
5. Legal placeholder voice: visible draft/compliance notes that should never appear to normal users.

The most urgent copy problem is not one bad phrase. It is a vocabulary system that has not been locked. The same product action is called "browser tab", "tab capture", "side panel", "in-page extension", "extension capture", and "any video on any page." The same verdict class appears as "No backing", "No Valid Backing", "UNVERIFIABLE", and "UNVERIFIED." The same Yentl summary appears as "Yentl's read" and "Yentl's take."

The source picker also contains the exact problem the user called out:

Current:

- Eyebrow: "Best for any page"
- Title: "Analyze a video I can play"
- Body: "Open Yentl beside any video, livestream, class, podcast, or embedded player."
- CTA: "Open side panel"

That reads like a literal capability note, not product copy. It is grammatically awkward because "a video I can play" centers the user's ability to play the video instead of the object Yentl is analyzing. It also hides the actual feature: tab capture.

Recommended primary replacement:

- Eyebrow: "Recommended for web video"
- Title: "Analyze the video on this page"
- Body: "Keep the player open while Yentl listens, transcribes, and checks what is being said."
- CTA: "Use tab capture"

Alternative punchier option:

- Eyebrow: "Works where captions do not"
- Title: "Listen to the playing tab"
- Body: "For livestreams, classes, podcasts, embedded players, and captionless videos."
- CTA: "Set up tab capture"

## Copy Principles To Lock

Yentl should sound like a sharp analyst sitting beside the user, not like a model card, compliance memo, or prototype dashboard.

Recommended voice:

- Plain, quick, concrete.
- Serious without being stiff.
- Skeptical without sounding smug.
- Transparent without naming every internal system.
- Lightly witty only when it reduces anxiety or sharpens meaning.

Use these core nouns consistently:

- `video`
- `tab`
- `transcript`
- `claim`
- `source`
- `marker`
- `report`
- `session`

Avoid or heavily limit:

- "surfaced"
- "tucked into the rhetoric"
- "evidence anchors"
- "engagement gate"
- "refuse silently"
- "functional sample"
- "proven replay"
- "server is not configured"
- "Deepgram" in normal user flow
- provider names like "Grok" in ordinary UI unless the product intentionally brands them
- "AI-generated" as a crutch everywhere; disclose it, but do not make the interface sound apologetic

### 7. Verdict vocabulary is inconsistent and confusing

Locations:

- `components/session/chips.tsx`
- `components/session/claim-detail.tsx`
- `components/session/claim-row.tsx`
- `components/session/filtered-list.tsx`
- `lib/client/filter-selectors.ts`
- `app/methodology/page.tsx`
- `components/verdict/VerdictCard.tsx`

Current labels:

- "No backing"
- "No Valid Backing"
- "NO BACKING"
- "UNVERIFIABLE"
- "UNVERIFIED"
- "No valid backing found"

Why it is a problem:

This is one of the most important concepts in the app. Users need to know whether Yentl found no evidence, cannot verify the claim, or has not finished checking. The current copy blurs those states.

Recommended vocabulary:

- In-progress: "Checking"
- Enough evidence: "Supported"
- Partly supported: "Mixed"
- False/misleading: "False"
- No reliable support found: "No reliable backing"
- Not a factual claim: "Opinion"

If the enum must stay `UNVERIFIABLE`, hide that word from normal users.

## Redundancy And Placement Notes

1. The phrase family around "biases and fallacies tucked into the rhetoric" appears in the landing page and metadata. It is too ornate to repeat. Use it zero or one time, then move to clearer product language.

2. "Yentl's read" is promising. Keep it if the brand wants a little character. But do not also use "Yentl's take." Pick one.

3. The app over-explains internal systems where users need action guidance. The user needs "connect to this tab", not "extension attach"; "import captions", not "evidence anchors"; "checking claims", not "feeding to fact-checker."

4. Legal/trust pages are too dense for first-read comprehension. Keep the formal material, but add one plain-language sentence at the top of each page.

5. Validation/demo copy should not share space with primary user flows. If the sample system must remain available, call it "Try a sample" for users and "Validation sample" for internal screens.

## Final Copywriter Verdict

The app is close to having a distinctive voice, but it needs a vocabulary lock and a ruthless cleanup of internal language. The highest-value copy pass should start with the source picker, browser-tab flow, extension panel, verdict labels, and visible trust/legal placeholders.

The product should say what it does in one clean line:

Yentl checks what is being said.

Then every button, status, and empty state should support that idea without sounding like a demo harness, legal memo, or model integration log.
